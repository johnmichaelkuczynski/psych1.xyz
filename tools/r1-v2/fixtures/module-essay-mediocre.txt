The Stanford Prison Experiment was done by Zimbardo in 1971 at Stanford University. He had some college students play prisoners and other ones play guards in a basement. The guards became mean very quickly and the prisoners became sad. Zimbardo had to stop the experiment after six days because it was getting too intense.

This shows that people will act differently when they are put into a role. The guards were not bad people before but the situation made them act bad. This is called situationism. It is the opposite of saying someone is just a bad person, which is called dispositionalism.

A lot of people use this experiment to talk about prisons in general and how guards can be cruel. But there are some problems with the study. The sample was only male college students so it might not apply to everyone. Also Zimbardo was the one running the prison so he might have influenced what the guards did.

In conclusion the Stanford Prison Experiment is a famous study that shows that situations matter for behavior. It has been very influential in social psychology even though there are some criticisms of it.
